Set Achievable Goals

Start by reducing the number of drinks:
If you usually drink every day, aim to have alcohol only on the weekends. Or, reduce the amount of alcohol by one drink each time.
Use the SMART method:
Specific: "I will drink only on Fridays."
Measurable: "I will limit myself to 2 drinks on Fridays."
Achievable: "I can manage this because I have support and an action plan."
Relevant: "This goal is important because I want better health."
Time-bound: "I will review my progress every two weeks."
Track your progress: Use a journal or an app to monitor how well you're sticking to your goals. Adjust the plan if necessary to make it more manageable.
