Identify Your Triggers

Write down when and where you drink:
Over the next week, keep a small notebook or use your phone to note the time, location, and emotion you feel before you decide to drink. This will help you identify patterns.
Analyze your notes: At the end of the week, look for common themes. Do you drink when you're stressed, bored, or socializing? Understanding these triggers is the first step to changing your behavior. Avoid or prepare for triggers:
For example, if you drink at parties, plan to attend without alcohol, or carry a non-alcoholic drink in hand. If stress is a trigger, try meditation or deep breathing exercises instead.
